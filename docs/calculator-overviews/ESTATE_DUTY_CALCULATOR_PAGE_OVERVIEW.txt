This document describes the Estate Duty Calculator page: purpose, structure,
components, and how it fits into the AS Brokers site. The page estimates
estate duty, executor fees, and total cash the estate will need at death.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE & USER JOURNEY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The /estate-duty-calculator page has three jobs:

  • Educate: Hero "Understand the cost of dying." Calculator estimates estate
    duty, executor fees, and total estate costs so families don't find out
    the hard way.
  • Context: Editorial explains what the estimate tells you (SARS potential
    claim, 20%/25% rates, cash payable before heirs); "The Hidden Problem"
    (growth increases tax bill, liquidity pressure); "A Practical Solution"
    (annual donations R100k/R200k, redirecting growth out of estate).
  • Convert: Next-step CTA to /annual-estate-reduction-strategy ("Calculate
    your annual estate reduction strategy"). Quick links to WhatsApp and
    solutions.

Linked from the Calculators hub under "Tax & Estate Exposure" (Phase 2). Part
of the estate-planning funnel: duty estimate → reduction strategy → contact.

5. CALCULATOR & EDITORIAL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  • Calculator: EstateDutyCalculator, max-w-4xl. Inputs typically estate
    value and structure; outputs estate duty, executor fees, total costs
    (high-level estimate; purpose is awareness, not precision).
  • What This Tells You: Figure is high-level estimate of potential SARS
    claim; duty on total value at death (property, investments, policies,
    etc.) after deductions; 20% first R30m, 25% above; payable in cash
    before heirs. "This calculator is not designed for precision. Its purpose
    is awareness."
  • The Hidden Problem: Growth inside estate increases tax over time;
    insufficient liquidity forces sales, delays, disruption; duty exposure can
    be reduced legally and gradually with early, consistent planning.
  • A Practical Solution: Annual donations allowance R100k per person / R200k
    per couple per year tax-free; redirect growth outside estate (e.g. trust);
    "planning signal" not prediction; solution requires structured action.
