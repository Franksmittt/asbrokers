## 1. Visual Strategy: "The Private Vault"

Move away from the current blue/white corporate look. Instead, aim for a **"Dark Mode Premium"** aesthetic that feels like a high-end banking app (think *Stripe* or *Apple Card*).

* **Glassmorphism:** Use frosted-glass cards for service descriptions. This looks incredible in Next.js with Tailwind CSS.
* **Micro-interactions:** Use **Framer Motion** for subtle entrance animations. As the user scrolls to "Retirement Planning," the numbers in a sample graph should count up dynamically.
* **High-Contrast Accents:** Stick to a deep charcoal/navy background with "vibrant electric blue" or "gold" accents for CTAs. This mimics the Samsung "Infinity" display vibe.

---

## 2. The "Bells & Whistles" (Feature Set)

To get leads, the site needs to be more than a digital brochure; it needs to be a **tool**.

### **A. Interactive "Retirement Run-Out" Calculator**

Since their current site highlights a "Bold Claim" about money running out, build a bespoke, high-performance calculator.

* **UX:** Instead of typing numbers, use sleek sliders (like Samsung’s UI).
* **Result:** A dynamic graph that shows the "Cliff" (when funds hit zero).
* **Lead Gen:** To get the full PDF breakdown of their result, they enter their email.

### **B. Service "Bento Grid"**

Replace long lists with a **Bento Box** layout (very Apple-inspired).

* Each service (Life Insurance, Estate Planning, etc.) is a tile of a different size.
* Hovering over a tile reveals a "Quick Summary" without leaving the page.

### **C. The "Client Portal" Gateway**

Even if you don't build a full backend yet, have a prominent, sleek "Client Login" button. It signals that they are a tech-forward firm.
