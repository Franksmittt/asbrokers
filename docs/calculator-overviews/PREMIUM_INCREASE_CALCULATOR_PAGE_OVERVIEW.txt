This document describes the Premium Increase Problem calculator page: purpose,
structure, components, and how it fits into the AS Brokers site. The page
exposes long-term cost of escalating life insurance premiums and supports
comparison of policies year by year.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE & USER JOURNEY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The /premium-increase-calculator page has four jobs:

  • Agitate: Hero "The Premium Increase Problem" – affordable today, but cost
    in 10, 15, 20 years? Cheapest policy today can become most expensive to
    keep.
  • Educate: Calculator requires manual entry of actual premiums from policy
    or quote (deliberate); editorial explains what usually goes wrong (low
    starting premium, 5% increase, guarantees that expire), why this
    calculator is different, behaviour-linked products, BrightRock
    comparison, how to use properly, who it's ideal for.
  • Answer: "The Real Question This Calculator Answers" – "Can I still afford
    this policy in 10, 15, or 20 years not just today?"
  • Convert: "Take the Next Step" – help interpreting results or policy
    sustainability; "Request a policy review below" → /contact. Quick links
    (WhatsApp, Home, Retirement income, Insurance & risk, Business risk,
    Estate planning).

Linked from the Calculators hub under "Life Insurance & Risk Planning" (Phase 2:
Secondary Risk Architecture) and from /solutions/life-insurance.

5. CALCULATOR & EDITORIAL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  • Calculator: PremiumComparisonCalculator. max-w-6xl (wider than other
    calculator pages). User enters actual premiums per year from policy/quote
    (no estimation); compare year-by-year, see total cost and acceleration.
  • The Premium Increase Problem: Most people buy on one number (starting
    premium); how premium changes over time often not shown; calculator
    exposes common costly issue – escalation faster than clients expect.
  • What Usually Goes Wrong: Sold on low starting premium with "5% per year",
    "guaranteed for period", "standard practice"; not always clear: scheduled
    escalation + age-rating, actual increase higher than quoted, guarantees
    expire after 10–15 years, premiums jump at review; red "✗" bullets for
    these points. "Policies become unsustainable when cover needed most."
  • Why This Calculator Is Different: Does not estimate; requires manual
    entry of actual premiums. Allows: real rand cost over time, compare
    insurers year by year, identify acceleration, total cost not just
    starting price. "Turns abstract escalation into real numbers."
  • Behaviour-Linked: Not all escalation bad; behaviour-linked structures
    can work for right client (high earners, wellness participation, engaged
    with benefits). Problem when wrong client or long-term behaviour not
    understood. "Fit and sustainability, not criticism of insurers."
  • BrightRock: Often referenced because it shows actual future premium
    amounts, year by year; transparent. Traditional products describe
    percentages while actual premiums diverge (age-rating, review events);
    calculator makes differences visible.
  • How to Use: Take schedule/quotation; enter actual monthly premium per
    year; compare side-by-side. Review year-by-year increases, long-term
    affordability, total premiums. "Goal is not lowest premium; avoid
    unpleasant surprises later."
  • Who It's Ideal For: Reviewing existing policies; replacing/upgrading
    cover; high earners comparing structures; business owners long-term need;
    advisers and clients wanting transparency.
  • The Real Question: "Can I still afford this policy in 10, 15, or 20 years
    not just today?" – matters more than starting premium.
