  AS BROKERS — CRM & CLIENT PORTAL
  What it does, features, benefits, how it works

Last updated: March 2026

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. WHAT IT IS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The CRM (Customer Relationship Management) and Client Portal are an authenticated
extension of the AS Brokers website. They give:

  - **Staff and owners** a single place to manage leads, clients, tasks, and
    correspondence, with role-based visibility (owner sees everything; staff
    see only what’s assigned to them).

  - **Clients** a secure place to see their wealth summary, documents, and
    messages with their advisor, and to upload FICA/documentation.

Currently the system runs on **mock data and mock authentication** (cookie-based
role/staff selection). It is built so that a real backend (Supabase, RLS, Magic
Links, HubSpot sync) can replace the mocks without changing the flows or UI.

Entry point: from the main site, click **“CRM & Client Portal”** in the nav, or
go to **/login**. Choose role (Admin / Staff / Client), then sign in.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. WHO USES IT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - **Albert (owner / admin)**  
    Full visibility: all leads, all clients, all tasks, all correspondence.  
    Can reassign any lead to any advisor (mock dropdown in lead detail).  
    Uses the same CRM as staff but with no filtering.

  - **Staff (advisors, underwriters, operations)**  
    Each staff member signs in as themselves (e.g. Johnny Farinha, Petro
    Vermeulen). They only see:
    - Leads assigned to them  
    - Clients assigned to them  
    - Tasks assigned to them  
    - Correspondence and notes for those leads/clients  

  - **Clients**  
    After signing in as “Client”, they see only the **Client Portal**: dashboard,
    documents, and messages. No access to CRM or other clients’ data.

3. CRM — FEATURES & BENEFITS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Dashboard (/crm)
  - Pipeline counts by stage: New, Contacted, Qualified, Proposal, Won, Lost.
  - Counts are role-based: owner sees all; staff see only their leads.
  - Recent leads table with links to lead detail and to Kanban / full Leads list.
  - Quick link to Kanban view.

Kanban (/crm/kanban)
  - Pipeline view: columns for each stage (New → Contacted → Qualified →
    Proposal → Won → Lost).
  - Drag-and-drop cards between columns (mock only; state resets on refresh).
  - Each card shows: name, intent, service category, Fit/Eng score, and (for
    owner) assigned advisor.
  - Clicking a name opens the lead detail page.
  - Benefits: visual pipeline, prioritisation, and (when backend is connected)
    status updates in one place.

Leads list (/crm/leads)
  - Table of leads with: Name (link to detail), Contact, Service, Intent,
    Status, Assigned advisor, Fit / Engagement score.
  - Role-filtered: owner sees all; staff see only assigned.
  - Service tags align with AS Brokers offerings (Retirement & Everest,
    Short-Term Personal/Business, Life Personal/Business, Medical & Wellness,
    Estate & Business, Claims).

Lead detail (/crm/leads/[id])
  - Full contact info, intent, status, service, Fit/Eng.
  - **Reassign advisor** (owner only): dropdown to assign another staff member
    (mock; no persist yet).
  - **Correspondence**: chronological thread (email, portal, WhatsApp) for that
    lead. Same thread appears on the client side when the lead is converted.
  - **Notes**: internal notes by author and date.
  - **Tasks**: tasks linked to this lead (or to the client once converted).
  - Benefits: one place for everything about a prospect; clear handover and
    history for the team.

Clients list (/crm/clients)
  - Converted clients (signed/onboarded) with portal access.
  - Columns: Name (link to detail), Contact, Services, Advisor, Converted date.
  - Role-filtered like leads.

Client detail (/crm/clients/[id])
  - Contact info, services, assigned advisor, WhatsApp link to advisor.
  - Same **correspondence**, **notes**, and **tasks** as on the lead side so
    the relationship is continuous from lead to client.
  - Benefits: full client view for the advisor; no silos between “lead” and
    “client” history.

Tasks (/crm/tasks)
  - List of tasks in two blocks: **To do** and **Completed**.
  - Each task: title, due date, assignee, and link to related lead or client.
  - Role-filtered: owner sees all; staff see only tasks assigned to them.
  - Benefits: follow-ups, FICA requests, and reviews in one place; no tasks
    lost in email.

Services (data model)
  - Every lead and client is tagged with one or more **service categories**:
    Retirement & Everest, Short-Term Personal, Short-Term Business, Life
    (Personal), Life (Business), Medical & Wellness, Estate & Business, Claims.
  - Used in lists and Kanban; ready for filtering and intent-based routing
    when the backend is connected (e.g. commercial liability → Petro, life
    onboarding → Sharine).

4. CLIENT PORTAL — FEATURES & BENEFITS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Wealth dashboard (/portal)
  - **My Advisor** card: advisor name, role, and one-tap **WhatsApp** and Call.
  - Portfolio summary: total value and monthly income (projected) from mock
    holdings.
  - **Holdings** list: e.g. 12.8% Strategic Income, short-term, life; values,
    monthly income, maturity where relevant.
  - **Charts** (Recharts): cumulative income over 60 months (mock); annuity
    drawdown / capital over time (mock).
  - Benefits: one screen for “where I stand” and who to contact; aligns with
    the blueprint’s “unified wealth dashboard”.

Documents (/portal/documents)
  - List of documents (FICA, quotes) from mock data.
  - **Upload**: client can choose a file (PDF/image). Upload is simulated:
    the file is not stored; the UI shows it as “Uploaded (mock)” and notes
    that real storage will use Supabase + audit trail.
  - Benefits: single place for client to upload FICA and see what’s on file;
    when backend is connected, advisor sees the same documents and audit log.

Messages (/portal/messages)
  - **Unified thread**: all messages (portal, email, WhatsApp) in one
    chronological list, matching the thread visible in CRM for that client.
  - **Reply** box: client can type and click “Send (mock)”. Button shows
    “Sent (mock)”; real sending and persistence will come with the backend.
  - Benefits: one conversation history for client and advisor; no need to
    switch between email and WhatsApp to see context.

5. HOW IT WORKS (CURRENT — MOCK)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Authentication
  - **Login** (/login): user chooses role (Admin, Staff, Client) and optionally
    a name. For Staff, they also choose which team member they are (dropdown of
    Albert’s team, excluding Albert).
  - Cookies set: mock-crm-role, mock-crm-user, mock-crm-staff-id (for admin/staff).
  - **Admin** is always treated as staff id “s1” (Albert). **Staff** use the
    selected id (e.g. s5 = Johnny). **Client** has no staff id.
  - **Log out**: Server Action clears cookies and redirects to /login.
  - No passwords; no Supabase Auth yet. This is for development and demos only.

Authorization (visibility)
  - **CRM**: If role is admin, all leads/clients/tasks are shown. If role is
    staff, only records where assigned_advisor_id (or assignedToId for tasks)
    matches the current user’s staff id are shown.
  - **Portal**: Only role === client can access /portal; otherwise redirect to
    /crm.
  - **Lead/Client detail**: If the user is staff, they can only open a lead or
    client that is assigned to them; otherwise 404.

Data (mock)
  - **lib/mock-crm.ts**: MOCK_LEADS, MOCK_CLIENTS, MOCK_STAFF, MOCK_CORRESPONDENCE,
    MOCK_TASKS, MOCK_NOTES. Service categories and labels. Helpers:
    getLeadsForAdvisor, getClientsForAdvisor, getCorrespondenceForLead/ForClient,
    getNotesForLead, getTasksForLead/ForClient/ForAdvisor, getCrmStats(advisorId?).
  - **lib/mock-portal.ts**: MOCK_HOLDINGS, MOCK_DOCUMENTS, MOCK_MESSAGES;
    getPortalAdvisor(), getPortfolioSummary(), chart data for Recharts.
  - **lib/mock-auth.ts**: getMockSession(), setMockSession(), clearMockSession(),
    canAccessCrm(), canAccessPortal(); cookie names and max age.

Navigation
  - **Main site**: On /crm, /portal, or /login the main Nav shows a reduced bar
    (logo, “Back to site”, and “Switch account” to /login when not on /login).
  - **CRM**: Sidebar (desktop) and top links (mobile): Dashboard, Kanban, Leads,
    Clients, Tasks; user/role and Log out; “Back to site” to /.
  - **Portal**: Top nav: Dashboard, Documents, Messages; user name; Log out;
    “Site” to /.

6. HOW IT WILL WORK (BACKEND PLANNED)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - **Auth**: Supabase Auth with Magic Links for clients; email/password or
    SSO for staff/admin. profiles table with role (admin, staff, client) and
    optional staff_id. RLS so clients only see their own rows.
  - **Data**: Supabase (or existing PostgreSQL) tables: leads, clients,
    correspondence, tasks, notes, documents (metadata + Supabase Storage for
    files). RLS: admin sees all; staff see where assigned_advisor_id = auth;
    client see where client_id = auth.
  - **HubSpot**: Bi-directional sync so lead/contact updates in the CRM update
    HubSpot and vice versa; lead scoring (Fit/Engagement) can drive routing.
  - **Documents**: Supabase Storage bucket with RLS; audit log for upload/view
    for FAIS/POPIA.
  - **Quoting**: Server Actions (or Trigger.dev jobs) for Everest/Amethyst
    quotes and PDF generation; link quotes to leads/clients.
